Mount Everest

Mount Everest, known as "Sagarmatha" in Nepal and "Chomolungma" in Tibet, is the tallest mountain on Earth. It rises to a staggering 8,848 meters (29,029 feet) above sea level, located in the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and Tibet. Every year, climbers from all over the world attempt to reach its summit, facing extreme weather conditions and physical challenges.

The mountain symbolizes adventure, perseverance, and nature's grandeur. It has a rich cultural and spiritual significance for the people living around it. Despite the challenges, conquering Everest remains a dream for many mountaineers and adventurers worldwide.
