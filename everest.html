Everest

Mount Everest, known as Sagarmatha in Nepali and Chomolungma in Tibetan, is the world's highest peak, towering at 8,848.86 meters. It is located in the Mahalangur Range of the Himalayas and lies on the border between Nepal and Tibet.

Everest is not just a mountain; it's a symbol of human ambition and endurance. Every year, thousands of climbers and trekkers visit the Everest region to experience its majestic beauty and challenge themselves.

Tourism

The Everest region attracts adventure seekers from around the globe. Popular activities include trekking to Everest Base Camp, scaling the peak itself, and exploring the rich culture of the Sherpa people who inhabit the area.

Things to Know

  • Best Time to Visit: March to May and September to November
  • Entry Fee:
    • Sagarmatha National Park Permit: NPR 3,000 for SAARC nationals, NPR 6,000 for others

Things to Do

  • Embark on the Everest Base Camp trek.
  • Climb Mount Everest or nearby peaks like Lhotse and Ama Dablam.
  • Explore Sherpa villages and Buddhist monasteries.
  • Witness the stunning views of the Himalayas, including sunrise at Kala Patthar.
