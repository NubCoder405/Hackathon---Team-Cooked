Kathmandu Durbar Square

The Kathmandu Durbar is a historic palace complex located in the heart of Kathmandu, Nepal. It served as the royal palace of the Nepalese monarchy from 1770 to 1886. The complex includes several buildings, courtyards, and temples, showcasing a mix of Malla, Shah, and Rana dynasties' architectural styles. Today, the Kathmandu Durbar is a popular tourist destination and a significant cultural landmark, offering insights into Nepal's rich history and heritage. Visitors can explore the palace's museums, gardens, and temples, including the stunning Hanuman Dhoka, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Basantapur refers to several places in Nepal, including a notable area in Kathmandu known for its historic architecture and cultural significance. It is also the name of a village development committee in Sunsari District, located in the Kosi Zone of southeastern Nepal.
