Krishna Mandir

Krishna Mandir, built between 1631 and 1637, is a 17th-century Shikhara-style temple located in Patan Durbar Square, Nepal. It was constructed by King Siddhi Narsing Malla and is recognized as a significant historical and architectural site, contributing to the area's status as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The Krishna Mandir in Patan is a 17th-century Hindu temple located in Patan, Gujarat, India. The temple is a significant example of Mughal architecture and is dedicated to Lord Krishna. Built in 1669, the temple features intricate carvings, ornate stone work, and a unique blend of Hindu and Islamic styles. It is considered one of the most beautiful temples in India and attracts tourists and pilgrims from all over the world. The temple is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized for its cultural and historical significance.
