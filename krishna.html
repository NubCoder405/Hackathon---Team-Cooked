Krishna Temple

About Krishna Mandir

Krishna Mandir, located in Patan Durbar Square, is a stunning 17th-century Shikhara-style temple. Built in 1637 by King Siddhi Narsing Malla, the temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna. It stands as one of the finest architectural masterpieces in Nepal and is a prominent UNESCO World Heritage Site.

This historic temple, made entirely of stone, features intricate carvings and a three-tiered structure that reflects Mughal and Hindu architectural styles. It holds deep cultural significance for Hindus and is a major destination for pilgrims and tourists alike. The temple stands as a testament to Nepal's rich heritage and craftsmanship.
